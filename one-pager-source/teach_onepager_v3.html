The TEACH Framework

AI should enhance, not replace, intentional instruction, professional judgment, and district-approved curriculum.

T
T

Thoroughly Evaluate

  • Review all AI-generated content for accuracy, bias, and appropriateness.
  • Ensure materials align to grade level and student needs.
  • Use professional judgment before using anything with students.
E
E

Engage with Curriculum

  • Align AI use to standards, curriculum, and pacing guides.
  • Support your learning intentions and success criteria.
  • Prioritize district-adopted instructional resources.
A
A

Adhere to Approved Tools

  • Approved tools meet privacy, security, and compliance standards.
  • They protect student data and PII far more completely than public tools.
C
C

Commit to Safe & Ethical Use

  • Never enter student PII: names, IEP or 504 details, grades, or photos.
  • Use anonymized prompts. Describe the student, never name them.
  • Follow FERPA and WCSD policy.
H
H

Harness the Potential of AI

  • Differentiate instruction to meet diverse learner needs.
  • Increase engagement, creativity, and accessibility for all students.
  • Support college, career, and future readiness.

Human Agency Scale

Agency means how much of the thinking stays with you. This scale says when to lean on AI; the stoplight below says what is allowed.

1
Fully HumanYou do the work. The right call when the thinking is the point.
2
Human-Led, AI SupportAI brainstorms and drafts; you direct and decide. Most teacher work lives here.
3
AI-Led, Human-ReviewedAI produces near-final work; you inspect every line before it counts.
4
Fully AI: not allowedNot allowed. If you didn't review it, it doesn't represent you.

The stoplight, in one glance

Green, go:lesson ideas, leveling texts, rubrics, exit tickets, rewording directions, sub plans.
Yellow, judgment:AI-built lessons, assessments, and feedback drafts. Strip names, apply T and E first.
Red, never:student PII, rosters or grades, IEP decisions, and any unapproved tool.
